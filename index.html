Research Experience

  • Data Science Intern @ Center for Computational Biology and Bioinformatics, Indiana University School of Medicine

    May 2022 - Aug 2022

    Advisor: Dr. Yunlong Liu

    My project was to use machine learning methods to discover the functional 3'-untranslated region variants' implication on substance use disorders (problematic alcohol use, opioid use disorder, cannabis use disorder). Our goal was to simultaneously use information about the variant and the surronding nucleotides, thus possibly a multi-task machine learning model, to predict how the variant will change the gene expression level of regulatory elements. I designed a multi-task neural network pipeline to predict the gene expression level efficiently and provided insights to regulate neurological disorder. Our model's prediction achieved strong spearman correlation coefficient in both predicting novel 3'-UTR sequence and variant's activity levels.

  • Undergraduate Research Assistant @ Connected and Automated Vehicle Highway Group, University of Wisconsin-Madison

    Jan 2022 - May 2022

    Advisor: Dr. Bin Ran

    CAVH group's CARLA project aimed to deploy the simulation of the roadside-centric connected autonomous driving system (CADS). To be more specific, our group expected to build multiple feasible subsystems, including Sensing and prediction, Planning and control, and Map/Traffic systems. I worked as the core member of the path planning group which focused on applying the planning algorithms to work on the macroscopic and microscopic path planning, motion planning, and behavior planning. I also researched on sampling-based searching algorithms, (such as RRT, BIT, RRT*) and deployed grid mapping system for collaborative automated driving in CARLA environment. Our further goal is to apply the control algorithms to control connected automated vehicles (CAV) at any vehicle intelligence level.

  • Undergraduate Research Assistant @ Reinforcement Learning Laboratory, University of Wisconsin-Madison

    Sep 2021 - Dec 2021

    Advisor: Dr. Josiah Hanna

    In fall 2021, I worked in the soccer robot project. Our team engineered the NAO (humanoid and programmable robot) to learn to score goals in a soccer game and also on the simulation soccer game platform. My task was to develop a binding framework that transmitted high-level neural network output from Python to low-level robot machines that coded in C++. I re-implemented the original C++ robot code in Python to better integrate it with reinforcement learning algorithms and accelerated the soccer robot transmitting speed to milliseconds level using Pybind11.

    Python
